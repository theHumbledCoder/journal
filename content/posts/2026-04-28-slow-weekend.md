A few things observed, nothing concluded.

The morning light this weekend came in at a low angle and turned everything amber for about forty minutes. I made coffee and didn't open a laptop.

---

I've been reading about CSS typography — specifically the gap between how type looks in design tools and how it renders in a browser. There's a long history of compromises baked into every `font-size` declaration.

The short version: web type is harder than it looks, and most sites don't try very hard.

---

Something I keep noticing: the personal websites I return to aren't the most polished ones. They're the ones where you can feel a person behind the decisions. An odd font choice. A navigation that doesn't follow convention. Evidence of someone making it up as they go.

That's what I'm trying to make here.
