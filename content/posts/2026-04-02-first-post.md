The honest answer is that I wanted a place that was mine in a way that Twitter and LinkedIn aren't.

Not because those platforms are bad, exactly. But because they're optimized for engagement — for keeping you on the platform, not for letting you think out loud. Every piece of writing gets measured against a metric.

A personal site has no metrics. No one's algorithm is deciding whether your post was good enough to show to people. You write something, it exists, that's the end of the story.

## What I plan to put here

- Notes on things I'm learning: web development, design, how software gets deployed
- Longer essays when I have something worth saying
- Photos, eventually
- Projects I'm working on

The ratio of these will probably shift over time. That's fine.

## What this is not

A newsletter. A brand. A "content strategy."

Just a place.
